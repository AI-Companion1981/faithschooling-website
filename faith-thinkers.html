"Traditional Arithmetic & Logic"

What Is Faith Thinkers?

Faith Thinkers is a traditional arithmetic and logic curriculum for Kindergarten through 5th Grade. Rather than relying on calculators, spiraling topics without mastery, or confusing new-math strategies, Faith Thinkers returns to what has always worked: drill for fluency, mastery before moving on, and understanding the "why" behind every operation.

Logic is woven throughout every grade level, training children to think in ordered, structured ways — a skill that benefits every subject. Faith integration connects mathematical truth to God's orderly creation, helping children see that math is a reflection of His character.

Faith Thinkers is designed for homeschool families — parent-led, mastery-based, and deeply effective.

Scope & Sequence by Grade

Click a grade to explore the Faith Thinkers curriculum for that year.

Kindergarten — Number Sense & Counting

Students build foundational number sense through hands-on activities, counting, ordering, and basic addition and subtraction concepts. Logic is introduced through patterns and simple sorting activities.

  • Counting to 100
  • Number recognition and writing 0–20
  • Ordering and comparing numbers
  • Introduction to addition (sums to 10)
  • Introduction to subtraction (within 10)
  • Patterns: shapes, colors, and numbers
  • Basic geometry: shapes and their names

1st Grade — Addition & Subtraction Mastery

First graders master addition and subtraction facts within 20 through daily drill and varied practice. Place value is introduced, and logic problems begin to develop reasoning skills.

  • Addition facts to 20 (mastery)
  • Subtraction facts within 20 (mastery)
  • Place value: tens and ones
  • Adding and subtracting two-digit numbers (no regrouping)
  • Measurement: inches and centimeters
  • Telling time to the half-hour
  • Logic: simple patterns and sequences

2nd Grade — Multi-Digit Operations

Students master regrouping in addition and subtraction, are introduced to multiplication concepts, and continue daily arithmetic drill. Logic puzzles increase in complexity.

  • Addition & subtraction with regrouping (3 digits)
  • Introduction to multiplication (2s, 5s, 10s)
  • Introduction to division concepts
  • Place value to 1,000
  • Money: counting coins and dollars
  • Time: to the nearest 5 minutes
  • Logic: simple word problems and reasoning

3rd Grade — Multiplication & Division Mastery

The hallmark year of Faith Thinkers: students achieve mastery of all multiplication and division facts through 12 via daily drill. Introduction to fractions and multi-step problem solving.

  • Multiplication facts 0–12 (full mastery)
  • Division facts 0–12 (full mastery)
  • Multi-step word problems
  • Introduction to fractions (halves, thirds, fourths)
  • Area and perimeter
  • Multi-digit multiplication (2-digit x 1-digit)
  • Logic: algebraic thinking and patterns

4th Grade — Fractions & Decimals

Fourth graders expand into fractions, mixed numbers, and introduction to decimals. Multi-digit multiplication and long division are mastered. Logic and problem-solving grow in sophistication.

  • Fractions: equivalent, comparing, adding & subtracting
  • Mixed numbers and improper fractions
  • Introduction to decimals (tenths and hundredths)
  • Multi-digit multiplication (2-digit x 2-digit)
  • Long division (1-digit divisor)
  • Introduction to geometry: angles, lines, symmetry
  • Logic: multi-step reasoning problems

5th Grade — Pre-Algebra Readiness

The capstone year prepares students fully for middle school math. Decimals, fractions, ratios, and introduction to variables build a complete pre-algebra foundation.

  • Operations with decimals (all four operations)
  • Operations with fractions (all four operations)
  • Ratios and rates
  • Introduction to variables and expressions
  • Order of operations
  • Coordinate planes and graphing
  • Logic: capstone reasoning and proof problems

Why Faith Thinkers Works

📋

Mastery-Based

Faith Thinkers does not move on until a concept is mastered. This builds a rock-solid foundation that prevents gaps later.

✎

Daily Drill

Arithmetic fluency requires repetition. Daily timed practice builds speed and accuracy, freeing mental resources for higher thinking.

🧠

Logic Integration

Logic is not a separate subject — it is woven through every grade. Students learn to reason, sequence, and problem-solve systematically.

☩

Faith-Connected

Math reflects the order and beauty of God’s creation. Every level draws connections between mathematical truth and Biblical worldview.
