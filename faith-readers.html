📚 Phonics & Reading

Faith Readers

A classical, systematic phonics program that grows confident, fluent readers — Kindergarten through 5th Grade.

"growing readers into leaders"

What Is Faith Readers?

Faith Readers is a complete K–5 reading and phonics curriculum built on a classical, systematic foundation. Rather than relying on sight words and guessing strategies, Faith Readers teaches children the code — how the English language actually works — so they can decode any word they encounter.

Every lesson is infused with faith-based content, reinforcing a Biblical worldview while developing strong literary skills. Students read decodable texts, learn spelling patterns, and build vocabulary rooted in truth and virtue.

Designed for homeschool families, Faith Readers is parent-led, easy to use, and effective from day one.

Scope & Sequence by Grade

Click a grade to see what your child will learn in Faith Readers that year.

Kindergarten — Foundations of Reading

Students are introduced to all 26 letters and their primary sounds, short vowels, consonant blends, and basic CVC (consonant-vowel-consonant) words. Instruction begins with oral phonemic awareness, then transitions to print.

  • Letter names & sounds (all 26 letters)
  • Short vowel sounds (a, e, i, o, u)
  • CVC word building and blending
  • Phonemic awareness activities
  • Introduction to decodable readers
  • Handwriting connection (print letters)

1st Grade — Phonics Mastery

First graders expand into consonant digraphs, long vowel patterns, vowel teams, and r-controlled vowels. They read longer decodable stories and begin writing sentences.

  • Consonant digraphs (ch, sh, th, wh, ph)
  • Long vowel silent-e patterns
  • Vowel teams (ai, ay, ee, ea, oa, ow)
  • R-controlled vowels (ar, er, ir, or, ur)
  • Decodable chapter books
  • Spelling rules & word families

2nd Grade — Fluency & Comprehension

Students solidify all phonics patterns and transition toward fluent, expressive reading. Comprehension strategies are introduced alongside continued phonics instruction.

  • Advanced vowel teams (oi, oy, ou, ow, ue, ew)
  • Silent letters and irregular patterns
  • Multi-syllable word decoding
  • Reading fluency and expression
  • Comprehension: main idea, sequence, inference
  • Beginning grammar: nouns, verbs, adjectives

3rd Grade — Word Study & Literature

Third graders study morphology, roots, prefixes, and suffixes to build vocabulary. They read longer literary works and begin responding to literature in writing.

  • Prefixes and suffixes
  • Greek and Latin roots
  • Syllabication rules (all 6 syllable types)
  • Vocabulary development
  • Reading comprehension strategies
  • Written response to literature

4th Grade — Literature & Rhetoric

Fourth graders read full literary works, develop analytical reading skills, and begin studying rhetoric and persuasive writing. All texts are curated for a Biblical worldview.

  • Full chapter book study
  • Literary elements (character, plot, theme)
  • Author's purpose & point of view
  • Advanced vocabulary & context clues
  • Introduction to paragraph writing
  • Faith-integrated discussion questions

5th Grade — Advanced Literacy

Fifth graders read across genres, write analytical responses, and develop the critical thinking needed for middle school and beyond. The capstone year of Faith Readers.

  • Multi-genre reading (narrative, informational, poetry)
  • Critical analysis and evidence-based response
  • Advanced grammar and mechanics
  • Research and note-taking skills
  • Essay writing introduction
  • Capstone literary project
