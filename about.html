🌿 Our Story

About FaithSchooling

We believe every child deserves an excellent education — one rooted in truth, grounded in faith, and built to last.

Our Mission

FaithSchooling exists to equip homeschool families with the tools, resources, and curriculum needed to provide a classical, faith-integrated education from Kindergarten through 5th Grade.

We believe that reading fluency and mathematical reasoning are two of the most important skills a child can develop in the early years — and that both are best taught systematically, masterfully, and through the lens of a Biblical worldview.

How FaithSchooling Began

FaithSchooling was born out of a simple but urgent need: families who wanted to homeschool their children with excellence and with faith — but who couldn’t find a curriculum that did both well.

Too many curricula either compromised on academic rigor in favor of faith content, or offered strong academics with no faith integration at all. We believed a better option was possible.

So we built it. Faith Readers and Faith Thinkers were designed from the ground up to be academically excellent, classically grounded, and naturally faith-integrated — without apology and without compromise.

“Education is not the filling of a pail, but the lighting of a fire.”

— W.B. Yeats

At FaithSchooling, we aim to light the fire — the love of reading, the joy of discovering how numbers work, and the wonder of learning as an act of worship.

Our Educational Philosophy

Three core convictions guide everything we create at FaithSchooling.

🌿

Classical Foundation

We draw on the classical tradition of education: systematic, ordered, and cumulative. Skills are built sequentially, and mastery is required before advancement.

☩

Biblical Worldview

All truth is God’s truth. Every subject — from phonics patterns to arithmetic facts — reflects the order, beauty, and logic of a Creator who made an intelligible world.

⭐

Excellence Without Compromise

We refuse to lower the bar. Children are capable of more than they are often given credit for, and they deserve materials that respect and challenge that capacity.
